Driver Licences

Authoritative driver licence records from all Australian states and territories — enabling nationally consistent identity verification, fraud prevention, and compliance checking.

What it is

NEVDIS holds driver licence records from all Australian states and territories. These records are shared between jurisdictions in real time and include the holder's licence status, class, conditions, and any restrictions or infringements. Demerit points and suspension data are also included where jurisdictions share these.

What it contains

Holder identity details, licence type and class, current status (current, cancelled, suspended, expired), endorsements and conditions, demerit points (where shared), and restriction and disqualification history.

Data quality

Records are supplied directly by state and territory road authorities. Privacy compliance checks and cross-verification with government identity registries ensure data integrity. Updates occur in real time as jurisdictions record changes.

Example uses

Licence verification, fraud prevention, cross-jurisdictional compliance checking, electoral roll updating (via AEC integration), and law enforcement roadside checking.

Terms of use

  • Information sourced from state and territory licensing authorities
  • Data provided 'as is' without guarantee of accuracy or completeness
  • Use limited to authorised verification purposes only
  • Privacy Act 1988 applies — personal information must be handled accordingly

How data flows

  1. When a jurisdiction issues or updates a driver licence, it shares the details with NEVDIS in real time.
  2. NEVDIS validates and stores the updated record.
  3. Authorised entities and other jurisdictions can query current licence status via NEVDIS.
  4. Changes to demerit points, suspensions, or cancellations are propagated across jurisdictions.
