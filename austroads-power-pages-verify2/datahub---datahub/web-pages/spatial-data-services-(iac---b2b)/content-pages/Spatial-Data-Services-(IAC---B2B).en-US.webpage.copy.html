Spatial Data Services (IAC / B2B)

Standardised spatial datasets derived from Geoscape layers — defining jurisdictional access rules and constraints for IAC and B2B systems for heavy vehicle operators.

What it is

TCA's Spatial Data Services provide standardised geospatial datasets that underpin the Intelligent Access Certification (IAC) and Bridge-to-Bridge (B2B) systems. These spatial layers define road network access rules, bridge constraints, route approvals, and jurisdiction boundaries for heavy vehicle operators seeking access to restricted routes.

Who uses it

Road authorities and jurisdictions defining access rules, heavy vehicle operators and fleet managers planning routes, telematics service providers integrating route compliance into onboard systems, and researchers conducting road network analysis.

Data sources

Spatial layers are derived from Geoscape's PSMA road network data, augmented with jurisdiction-supplied access rules, bridge data, and road condition information. TCA standardises and validates these layers before distribution.

Key datasets

DatasetDescription
IAC Road NetworkApproved routes for Intelligent Access Certification — with access conditions per vehicle class
B2B Spatial LayerBridge-to-Bridge spatial constraints — bridge load limits and height restrictions
Jurisdiction BoundariesState and territory boundary layers for route compliance and reporting
Road Segment Access RulesPer-segment access conditions including time-of-day and vehicle class restrictions

Terms of use

  • Spatial data provided for authorised IAC and B2B purposes only
  • Geoscape base data subject to Geoscape licensing terms
  • Redistribution of spatial layers requires TCA authorisation
  • Route approvals derived from spatial data do not replace legal permit obligations
