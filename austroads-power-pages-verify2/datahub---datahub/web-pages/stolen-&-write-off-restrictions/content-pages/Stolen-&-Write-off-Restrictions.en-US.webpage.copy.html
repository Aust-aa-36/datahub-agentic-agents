Stolen & Write-off Restrictions

National records of stolen vehicles and written-off vehicle restrictions (WOVR) — sourced from police and state registries to support fraud prevention and consumer protection.

What it is

NEVDIS maintains national records of reported stolen vehicles and vehicles subject to write-off restrictions (WOVR — Written-Off Vehicle Register). Police agencies report stolen vehicles to NEVDIS, and state registries report statutory write-offs and repairable write-offs.

What it contains

Stolen vehicle records (VIN, plate, report date, reporting jurisdiction), written-off status (statutory write-off or repairable write-off), and restrictions on re-registration. Both active and historical records are held.

Example uses

Pre-purchase vehicle checks, dealer compliance, insurance assessment, fraud detection, roadside enforcement, and consumer protection.

Terms of use

  • Data sourced from police agencies and state vehicle registries
  • Absence of a stolen or write-off record does not mean the vehicle is clear
  • Data provided 'as is' — not a guarantee of vehicle history
  • Use limited to verification and compliance purposes

How data flows

  1. Police agencies report stolen vehicles to NEVDIS via jurisdiction interfaces.
  2. State registries report statutory and repairable write-offs as they are recorded.
  3. NEVDIS consolidates records into the national stolen and WOVR dataset.
  4. Authorised users and other jurisdictions can query the national record in real time.
