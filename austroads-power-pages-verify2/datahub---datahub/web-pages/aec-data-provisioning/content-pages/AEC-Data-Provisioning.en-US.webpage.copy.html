AEC Data Provisioning

Daily scheduled extract of driver licence holder changes supplied to the Australian Electoral Commission — supporting electoral roll accuracy and automatic enrolment.

What it does

The AEC Data Provisioning product extracts changes to driver licence holder records from NEVDIS on a daily basis and supplies them to the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC). This enables the AEC to automatically update the electoral roll when licence holders move, change details, or newly qualify for enrolment.

Who uses it

The Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) is the sole recipient of this data product. The data feeds into the automatic enrolment and roll update processes under the Commonwealth Electoral Act.

Use cases

Automatic electoral roll updates for address changes, new enrolments of eligible Australians who obtain a driver licence, and roll maintenance following licence cancellations or holder deaths.

Process

  1. NEVDIS identifies all driver licence holder records changed in the previous 24 hours.
  2. A scheduled daily extract is generated containing relevant holder details (name, address, DOB, citizenship status where available).
  3. The extract is transmitted securely to AEC via agreed data sharing channels.
  4. AEC processes the extract to update the electoral roll, matching against existing enrolment records.

Terms of use

  • Governed by a formal data sharing agreement between Austroads and the AEC
  • Access restricted exclusively to the Australian Electoral Commission
  • Data used solely for the purpose of maintaining the Commonwealth electoral roll
  • Privacy Act 1988 and Commonwealth Electoral Act apply
