Spatiotemporal Data (Position & Speed)

GNSS telemetry capturing vehicle movement, speed, and positional accuracy at the trip level — the raw telematics data underpinning all TCA compliance and analytics products.

What it is

Spatiotemporal data is the core GNSS telemetry feed from heavy vehicles enrolled in TCA's National Telematics Framework. Each record represents a vehicle position at a point in time — capturing latitude, longitude, speed, heading, and satellite accuracy. Records are used for trip reconstruction, route compliance analysis, and as the foundation for all TCA road analytics products.

What it contains

Timestamped GNSS position records (latitude/longitude), vehicle speed, heading, HDOP (horizontal dilution of precision), trip identifiers, and device-level metadata. Data is recorded continuously while the vehicle is in operation.

Example uses

Route compliance checking for IAC and NHVR permits, speed monitoring, trip reconstruction, road usage analysis, freight network analytics, and infrastructure planning.

Terms of use

  • Governed by the National Telematics Framework and relevant jurisdiction legislation
  • Raw positional data accessible only to authorised compliance entities (TCA, jurisdictions)
  • Aggregated/anonymised derivatives available for analytics and planning purposes
  • Privacy obligations apply — data cannot be used to identify vehicle operators without authorisation

Access process

  1. Submit access request through TCA's authorised partner process.
  2. Eligibility and privacy compliance checks completed.
  3. Data sharing agreement executed.
  4. Secure API or extract access provisioned by TCA.
