Enabling RAV

Validates VINs against the Register of Approved Vehicles and shares approval data with state and territory registries — enabling ADR-compliant vehicles to be registered in Australia.

What it does

The Register of Approved Vehicles (RAV) is the official list of motor vehicles approved for supply in Australia under the Motor Vehicle Standards Act 2018. NEVDIS validates VINs against the RAV and shares approval status with state and territory registration authorities, enabling vehicles to be registered only if they meet Australian Design Rules (ADR) compliance requirements.

Who uses it

State and territory vehicle registration authorities, vehicle importers and compliance plate authorities, and the Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications and the Arts (DITRDCA) which administers the RAV.

Process

  1. DITRDCA approves a vehicle model for supply in Australia and records it on the RAV.
  2. NEVDIS receives RAV approval data and associates it with relevant VINs.
  3. When a jurisdiction attempts to register a vehicle, NEVDIS checks the VIN against the RAV.
  4. Registration proceeds only if the VIN is associated with a RAV-approved vehicle.

Terms of use

  • RAV data sourced from DITRDCA — the authoritative source for vehicle approvals
  • Jurisdictions access RAV validation via NEVDIS integration only
  • Individual VIN validation is provided in real time during registration processing
