High Productivity Freight Routes

Telematics-derived insights into the use of approved high-productivity freight vehicle routes — supporting route management and access decisions.

What it does

The HPFR (High Productivity Freight Routes) service analyses telematics data from B-double, road train, and other high-productivity freight vehicles to produce annual insights into how these vehicles actually use approved routes. The service helps road authorities understand route demand, identify access gaps, and prioritise route improvements.

Who uses it

Infrastructure planners, freight policy developers, road agencies, and researchers. Data is updated annually and reflects the previous year's vehicle movements.

Use cases

Route prioritisation for road upgrades, annual freight network reporting, evidence base for new route approval decisions, and assessment of route productivity and utilisation.

How it works

  1. Telematics records from high-productivity vehicles are filtered and matched to approved HPFR routes.
  2. Annual aggregation produces usage counts, productivity scores, and route metrics.
  3. Results are published to the HPFR ArcGIS dashboard with annual state-by-state breakdowns.
  4. Bulk annual datasets available to authorised users on request.

Terms of use

  • Data aggregated and anonymised — no identification of individual vehicles or operators
  • Productivity scores are relative to monitored vehicles only — not representative of the full freight task
  • Annual data — not updated in real time
  • Governed by TAP Terms and Conditions

Access process

  1. Submit access request via TCA TAP User Enrolment.
  2. Jurisdictional and privacy checks completed.
  3. Access provisioned to appropriate dashboard. Average approval: 2 business days.
