TCA · Data Product

New Zealand

Aggregated insights into historical heavy-vehicle movements across the New Zealand road network — supporting freight planning and road management through telematics-derived spatial analysis.

What it does

This service provides aggregated insights into the usage of New Zealand public roads by monitored heavy vehicles, from a collaborative study led by the National Road Carriers Association (NRC), in partnership with Waka Kotahi NZ Transport Agency (NZTA) and Austroads. The dashboard visualises historical telematics data from approximately 120 Restricted Access Vehicles (RAVs) to demonstrate how vehicle-generated data can support road management, freight planning, and network-level insight.

Road usage records are joined to an early version of the NZTA Asset Management Data Standard (AMDS) road network, enabling users to view spatial patterns, intensity of use, and segment-specific summaries.

Who uses it

Road managers, freight planners, partner organisations (NRC, NZTA, Austroads), and authorised research bodies. This is a historical study dataset — not a live operational service.

Use cases

  • Visualising the most frequently used freight routes within regions such as Auckland, Waikato, and Bay of Plenty
  • Identifying heavy-vehicle travel corridors that support freight and logistics operations
  • Assessing route importance or potential access needs for RAV vehicles
  • Using monthly variations in travel to understand seasonal or operational patterns

Access process

  1. Request access via TCA or the project's governance group (NRC/NZTA/Austroads).
  2. Eligibility check — road manager, planner, or partner organisation.
  3. Access provisioned in the ArcGIS Enterprise portal.

Terms of use

  • All data aggregated and anonymised — cannot be used to identify vehicles or operators
  • Dataset covers ~120 RAV vehicles — a sample, not the full freight task
  • Dataset is not updated — reflects a historical study period only
  • Intended use: planning, analysis, freight insight, and network understanding — not enforcement
  • Governed by TAP Terms and Conditions
